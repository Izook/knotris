About Knotris

The following is the result of an undergraduate research project conducted by Dr. Allison Henrich, Alexandra Ionescu, Brooke Mathews, Isaac Ortega, and Kele Tesfaye.

Knotris is a game based off of the popular game Tetris, which was created in 1984 by Alexey Pajitnov. Tetris is a fun puzzle game which requires players to rotate, slide, and drop tetris pieces onto the game grid in a way that fills as many horizontal rows as possible, without leaving any open spaces.

We began our research looking at knot mosaics and soon began to realize how easily they can be used to form puzzles and difficult questions. Naturally, we then progressed to the idea of creating a Tetris-like game using the 11 knot mosaic tiles. We developed a web and mobile app version of the game so we, and others we share our research with, are able to play it in real time. After constructing the game, we then went into investigating the difficulty of the game by looking at probabilities of scoring, given different game board orientations.

In this paper, we describe the process of designing the game, discuss strategies found using probability calculations to maximize score during game play, explain the difficulty and play ability of the game, and lastly, share the process of developing the mobile version of the game.

The game was developed using the Godot Game Engine.
